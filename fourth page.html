Corpse Flower

its putrid smell is most potent during peak bloom at night into the early morning.

  1. Appearance

    • The entire flower will have two main colors. The spadix, the center structure, will be olive green.

    • The inner portion of the spathe will be of a maroon color with a velvet-like appearance.

    • The actual flowers which are located at the base of the flower will also share similar colors

  2. Symbolism

    • In many cultures, it represents the exotic and the mysterious, owing to its rarity and the dramatic bloom that can take several years to manifest.

    • bolizes rebirth, decay, and the beauty that can come from decay. It also represents the allure of the rare and beautiful, and the idea that the spirit and its objects of desire are indistinguishable. Explanation Rebirth and decay The corpse flower represents the cycle of birth, death, fecundity, and decay.

  3. History

    • The plant was discovered in 1878.

    • The first cultivated bloom occurred in 1889.

    • Its first bloom in the Western Hemisphere was recorded in 1937.
